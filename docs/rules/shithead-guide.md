# 💩 Shithead — Complete Game Guide

## Overview

Shithead (also known as *Palace*, *Karma*, or *Shed*) is a shedding-type card game for **2–6 players**. The goal is simple: **be the first player to get rid of all your cards**. The last player left holding cards is the "Shithead" and loses the round.

---

## What You Need

- A standard 52-card deck (add a second deck for 5+ players)
- 2–6 players
- A flat surface to play on

---

## Card Rankings

Cards rank from **lowest to highest**:

```
3 < 4 < 5 < 6 < 7 < 8 < 9 < J < Q < K < A
```

> Special cards (2, 3, 7, 8, 10) follow different rules — see [Special Cards](#special-cards) below.

---

## Setup

1. **Deal each player 9 cards** from a shuffled deck.
2. Each player arranges their cards into three groups:
   - **3 face-down table cards** — placed in a row on the table. These cannot be looked at until later.
   - **3 face-up table cards** — placed on top of the face-down cards. All players can see these.
   - **3 hand cards** — kept in the player's hand, hidden from others.
3. The remaining cards form the **draw pile** in the centre.

---

## How to Play

### Turn Order
Play proceeds **clockwise**. One player starts by playing a card onto the discard pile. From then on, each player takes their turn in order.

### Playing a Card
- You must play a card that is **equal to or higher** than the top card of the discard pile.
- You may play **multiple cards of the same rank** in a single turn (e.g., three Kings). This applies to special cards too — you can play multiple 2s, 8s, etc. at once.
- If you **cannot play**, you must **pick up the cards from the discard pile** and add them to your hand.

### Drawing Cards
- After playing, if the draw pile still has cards and you have **fewer than 3 hand cards**, draw back up to 3.
- Once the draw pile is empty, you no longer draw.

### Progressing Through Your Cards
Players use their cards in this strict order:
1. **Hand cards** — played while the draw pile exists, and until the hand is empty after the draw pile runs out.
2. **Face-up table cards** — played once the draw pile is gone and your hand is empty.
3. **Face-down table cards** — played blind (no peeking!) once face-up cards are gone. Flip one card at a time; if it cannot be played, pick it up into your hand.

Once a player has no cards left, they are **finished** and out of the game.

---

## Special Cards

These cards have unique effects that override normal play:

| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **2** | Can be played on **any** card. The next player can play any card, **except a 7**. |
| **3** | Can be played on **any** card. It is **transparent** — the card played before the 3 is still considered active, so the next player must play on that earlier card as if the 3 wasn't there. |
| **7** | Can only be played on another 7 or a higher card. After a 7 is played, the next player must play a **7 or lower**. |
| **8** | Skips the next player's turn entirely. |
| **10** | Immediately **removes the entire discard pile** from the game. The player who played the 10 goes again. |
| **Four of a kind** | When four cards of the same rank end up on the discard pile (built up over consecutive turns), the pile is **removed from the game** and the player who completed the four goes again. |

---

## Winning & Losing

- The **first player to play all their cards** (hand + face-up + face-down) is finished and out of the game.
- Play continues until only **one player** remains with cards.
- That player is the **Shithead** 💩 and loses.

---

## Strategy Tips

- **Prioritise high cards face-up** on the table (Aces, Kings) so they're easier to play later.
- **Save your 2s and 10s** — they're lifesavers when the pile is stacked high.
- **Play low cards early** to clear them from your hand before the draw pile runs out.
- **Use 3s wisely** — playing a 3 keeps the previous card active, which can be a blessing or a curse depending on what was played before it.
- **Track what's been played** — knowing the remaining cards helps you judge when to burn the pile.
- **Don't waste a 10** on a low pile — save it for when the discard stack is dangerous to pick up.

## Common House Rules

Different groups often play with variations. Here are popular ones:

| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Suit matching** | Some groups require suit to match as well as rank. |
| **Shithead wears the crown** | The loser deals next round and is called "Shithead" until someone new loses. |
| **No picking up on last card** | If a player has one card left, they cannot be forced to pick up — must play or skip. |

## Quick Reference

```
Goal:         Be first to play all your cards
Card order:   Hand → Face-up table → Face-down table (blind)
Draw:         Draw back to 3 cards while the draw pile lasts
Play rule:    Match or beat the top discard card
Multiple:     You may play multiple cards of the same rank in one turn
Can't play:   Pick up the entire discard pile

Special cards:
  2  = play on anything, resets pile (7 cannot be played after a 2)
  3  = play on anything, transparent (previous card stays active)
  7  = next player must play 7 or lower
  8  = skip next player's turn
  10 = burn the discard pile, play again
  Four of a kind on pile = burn pile, play again

Loser:        Last player with cards = Shithead 💩
```
